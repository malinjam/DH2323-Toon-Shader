DH2323-Toon-Shader

Hello!
We, Ferran Campos Llopart (ferrancl@kth.se), Malin Malin Jauad Almeida Marques (malinjam@kth.se), and Maria Zelenika (zelenika@kth.se), are first-year master students studying Interactive Media Technology at KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm, Sweden.
We have created this blog as part of our project for the course Computer Graphics with Interaction (DH2323). Our project focuses on developing a toon-shader and conducting a perceptual study to analyze how different shapes influence the perception of glossiness. Through this blog, we aim to share insights about our project, including the techniques employed, our experiences, both the mistakes and successes, in reverse chronological order.
To access the code, Unity project, and this blog, please visit our Github repository.
You can download our report and specification.

Tough decisions

12 May, 2023

We were unable to find papers on toon-shading perception, ask professor for help through emails, defined our idea more firmly and got this great paper. This study does however not use a toon shader and renders different materials using information from a database (not publicly available). We are unable to find a similar database that provides material information for toon shaders. We think we can solve this by rendering different materials by recreating them by looking at objects from a videogame that uses toon shading.
